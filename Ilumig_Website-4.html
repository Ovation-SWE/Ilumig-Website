Management and Cutovers

Since a control system stays in place for many years for each version, it supports a grid that is constantly evolving and growing, it is imperative to think of engineering the system for the future in terms of the hardware and in terms of how the application functions as the grid grows.

With control systems, there tend to be numerous stakeholders such as field engineers, scada techs, communication engineers, Control room operators, transmission engineers, planners, distribution teams, compliance, Billing teams and they can be hard to identify and motivate. It is also very important to understand and categorize the type of stakeholder in terms of - are they a user of the system, do they only rely on archival data or maybe only issue data requests. We can help leave no one behind.

Control system upgrades can be incredibly complex and can span many years. It is really important to have line of sight to current system challenges and think about remediation for the critical ones to ensure system availability as the new system gets ready. We can help with sustainability efforts.

Foundation of success of the effort lies in gaining a deep understanding of what every stakeholder needs in the system for the company to thrive! It also lies in categorizing their needs into buckets identified while breaking down complexity to aid ultimate traceability.

Rarely is a control system able to meet the needs of business off the shelf. We can help shed light starting with what you need to succeed, what part of that is available in the system, what is missing, what changes need to move from the old system to the new, how can the gap be closed, to ensure as a business you have everything you need to succeed.
